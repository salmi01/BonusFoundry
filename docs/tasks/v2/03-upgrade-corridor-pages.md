# Goal 3 — Upgrade All Corridor Pages

## Context

Upgrade every corridor page using the AI Content Design System.

Reuse existing components.

Do not redesign the site.

---

## Objectives

Make every corridor page:

- more useful;
- less repetitive;
- easier to scan;
- easier for AI to extract.

Each corridor should have its own identity.

---

## Reduce Repetition

Many corridor pages currently repeat nearly identical wording.

Avoid repeating sentences like:

- compare fees
- compare delivery methods
- compare verification
- compare eligibility

Instead, highlight what matters most for that corridor.

Examples:

- Bank deposit
- Cash pickup
- Mobile money
- Fast delivery
- Exchange rate
- Popular providers
- Typical payout methods

---

## Apply Components

Use where relevant:

- Quick Answer
- Key Takeaways
- Corridor Summary
- Comparison Table
- Provider Cards
- Best For
- Common Mistakes
- Troubleshooting
- Mini FAQ
- Related Guides
- Related Providers
- Last Verified

---

## Add Comparison Tables

Each corridor should include a concise comparison table.

Example columns:

- Provider
- Delivery methods
- Typical speed
- Bonus available
- Best for

Do not duplicate provider pages.

Summarize and link.

---

## Improve Structure

Suggested order:

1. Quick Answer
2. Key Takeaways
3. Corridor Summary
4. Provider Comparison
5. Provider Cards
6. Best For
7. Common Mistakes
8. FAQ
9. Related Resources

---

## Editorial Rules

Keep corridor-specific content.

Avoid generic paragraphs copied across routes.

Mention only relevant payment methods, recipient options and bonus considerations for that corridor.

---

## Constraints

- Keep existing URLs.
- Preserve internal linking.
- Reuse components from Goal 1.
- Do not invent provider information.

---

## Deliverables

1. Corridor pages updated.
2. Repetitive content reduced.
3. Comparison tables added.
4. Components reused.
5. Build passes.
6. Lint passes.
