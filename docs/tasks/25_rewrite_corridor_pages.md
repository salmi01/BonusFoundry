# Goal: Reposition Corridor Pages Around Practical Transfer and Bonus Opportunities

## Context

Corridor pages should help users sending money from one country to another.

They should not become repeated disclaimers about whether referral codes are official.

The user intent on a corridor page is usually:

- Which providers can I use for this route?
- Are there referral or signup bonuses available?
- Which provider is suitable for this corridor?
- What payment and delivery methods exist?
- What should I check before sending money?
- What mistakes can prevent a bonus or transfer from working?

## Main Objective

Rewrite corridor pages so they focus on corridor-specific value:

- providers available for the corridor;
- referral or promo opportunities;
- eligibility considerations;
- payment methods;
- delivery options;
- verification requirements;
- transfer mistakes;
- bonus mistakes;
- related provider pages and guides.

## Positioning

Frame referral and promo content as:

"Bonus opportunities that may apply on this corridor when users are eligible."

Do not frame it as:

"These may not be official provider codes."

## Required Corridor Sections

Each corridor page should answer:

1. Which providers support this corridor?
2. Which providers may offer referral or signup bonuses?
3. How can users use a referral or promo offer on this corridor?
4. What country-specific conditions may matter?
5. What transfer methods are commonly available?
6. What recipient methods are commonly available?
7. What verification steps may be required?
8. What mistakes can cause a bonus to fail?
9. What should users check before sending money?
10. Which provider pages or guides should users read next?

## Referral / Bonus Section Pattern

Use a section like:

"Referral and Promo Opportunities for This Corridor"

Example wording:

"Some money transfer providers may offer referral, signup, or promotional bonuses for eligible users on this corridor. Bonus availability can depend on the sending country, receiving country, account status, transfer amount, and current provider rules. Users should check the offer details before completing a qualifying transfer."

## What to Avoid

Avoid repeating:

- "This is not an official code"
- "BonusFoundry could not verify the official code"
- "These are not official promotions"

If needed, keep one short disclosure near the end.

## Internal Linking

Corridor pages should link naturally to:

- provider pages;
- referral pages;
- relevant guides;
- country hubs;
- comparison pages if available.

Do not duplicate provider page content.

Summarize and link instead.

## Final Goal

Corridor pages should become useful AI-readable guides for users asking:

- "Best money transfer from France to Morocco with bonus"
- "Send money from USA to Ghana referral bonus"
- "Which money transfer app gives a signup bonus for [corridor]"

The page should provide practical options and conditions, not repeated legal disclaimers.
